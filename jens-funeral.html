The Funeral

3rd February 2023

On the evening of December 6th, 2022, the world lost Jen, a woman whose quiet strength and warm heart touched the lives of everyone she encountered. As the day faded into night, so too did Jen's time in this world, leaving behind a legacy of love, resilience, and cherished memories. Her passing marked the end of a chapter, yet her spirit lingers in the hearts of those who knew her, a gentle reminder of the profound impact one life can have.

Her death completely destroyed me, shattering my world in ways I could never have imagined. The unbearable task of informing our mutual friends, scattered across the vast expanse of the internet, weighed heavily on my heart. Each message I sent felt like another piece of my soul being torn apart, as I watched the shock and sorrow ripple through our tight-knit community. The bonds we had all shared seemed to unravel with every keystroke, leaving me feeling more isolated and broken with each passing moment. In those painful hours, the reality of her absence began to sink in, and I was left to grapple with the overwhelming grief of losing not just a friend, but a part of myself.

Amidst the overwhelming grief, I reached out to Isi, desperately asking her to share my contact information with Jen's parents. Although we had met before, our interactions had been brief, leaving us without a direct line of communication. I felt an urgent need to connect with them, to bridge the chasm of our shared loss, and perhaps find solace in recounting the memories of the vibrant woman we all cherished. Waiting for their response was an agonizing stretch of time, each silent moment amplifying the emptiness Jen's absence had carved into our lives.

When I received the invitation to Jen’s funeral, a wave of gratitude washed over me. Despite the unbearable pain of her loss, the thought of being able to gather with those who loved her, to say our final goodbyes together, brought a small sense of comfort. Knowing that I, along with our mutual friends, had been included in this deeply personal moment meant more than words could express. It was a chance to honor Jen's life, to share our grief, and to support one another in the face of such a profound loss. In that moment, I felt an overwhelming thankfulness that we were given the opportunity to be there, to pay our respects, and to find strength in each other as we mourned the woman who had touched all our lives so deeply.

In the days leading up to the funeral, I was overwhelmed with anxiety. The responsibility of arranging flights, booking hotels, and coordinating with everyone who would be arriving at the airport weighed heavily on me. I wanted everything to go smoothly, knowing how important it was for all of us to be there for Jen and each other. But amidst the logistics, a deeper worry gnawed at me — I had never attended a German funeral before, and I had no idea what to expect. The fear of not knowing the customs or how to properly pay my respects added another layer of stress. I was terrified of making a mistake, of somehow dishonoring the memory of someone who had meant so much to me. As the funeral approached, my panic grew, and I found myself barely able to sleep, consumed by the need to make sure everything was perfect for the farewell she deserved.

In the days before the funeral, I found myself unable to focus on work, consumed by a desperate need to prepare for what lay ahead. Instead of my usual tasks, I spent hours researching German funeral customs, trying to understand what might happen and what would be expected of me. A deep worry took root in my heart as I learned about the German preference for cremation, fearing that I might never see Jen again until the day of the service—if at all. The thought of not being able to say a final, personal goodbye to her filled me with dread. However, when we arrived at her family home and were greeted by Jen’s ashes, a wave of overwhelming gratitude swept over me. The intimate setting allowed us to be close to her one last time, to share our memories and grief in a space that had once been filled with her presence. It was a small comfort amidst the sorrow, a moment of connection that I hadn’t dared hope for.

After the funeral, we were invited to the wake, where I finally had the chance to meet Jen's entire family. Stepping into their home, I was filled with a mix of nervousness and gratitude, unsure of how they might receive us. But as we shared stories and memories, their warmth and kindness became immediately evident. They expressed how honored they were that we had traveled from overseas to be there for Jen, and their words of gratitude touched us deeply. Hearing them speak of how much our friendship had meant to her was incredibly moving, and it brought a sense of comfort to know that we had played a meaningful role in her life. In that moment, the distance and cultural differences melted away, replaced by a shared love for Jen that united us all.
